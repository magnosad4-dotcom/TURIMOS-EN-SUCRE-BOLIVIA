What is Cybersecurity?

Cybersecurity is a set of practices and technologies designed to protect systems, networks, and data from malicious digital attacks.

The importance of cybersecurity has grown exponentially in recent years. With increased digitalization, cyberattacks have become more sophisticated and frequent, affecting companies, governments, and individuals alike.

In today's digital era, protecting your personal, financial, and professional information is more critical than ever. Cybercriminals use advanced techniques such as phishing, ransomware, malware, and social engineering to compromise systems and steal valuable data.

Key Data:
• Over 4 billion records lost annually
• Average cost of a breach: $4.29 million USD
• Average detection time: 207 days

Major Cyber Threats

Learn about the most common threats and how to protect yourself from them.

High Threat

Phishing

A deception technique where trusted entities are impersonated through fraudulent emails to obtain sensitive data.

Common Methods:
• Fake bank emails
• Malicious links
• Identity spoofing
Critical Threat

Ransomware

Malware that encrypts important data and demands payment for recovery. Affects companies and governments massively.

Characteristics:
• File encryption
• Ransom demands
• Data exfiltration
High Threat

Malware

Malicious software designed to infiltrate systems and cause damage, steal information, or take control.

Types:
• Viruses
• Worms
• Trojans
Technical Threat

SQL Injection

An attack that injects malicious SQL code into forms to illegally access databases.

Impact:
• Data theft
• Loss of integrity
• Unauthorized access
High Threat

DDoS Attack

Floods a server with millions of requests to take it down and make it inaccessible to legitimate users.

Consequences:
• Service unavailability
• Economic losses
• Reputation damage
Critical Threat

Zero-Day Exploits

Unknown vulnerabilities exploited before the vendor can patch them.

Characteristics:
• Unknown vulnerabilities
• No patches available
• Very difficult to detect

Recommended Learning Path

Follow this progressive path from beginner to cybersecurity expert.

  • 1

    Cybersecurity Fundamentals Weeks 1-2

    Start by learning the basics: the CIA triad (Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability), types of threats, and defense-in-depth principles. Understand why cybersecurity is important today.

  • 2

    Identity & Access Management Weeks 3-4

    Learn to create secure passwords, implement multi-factor authentication (MFA), manage sessions, and understand OAuth and SAML. These are the pillars of personal defense.

  • 3

    Network & System Security Weeks 5-7

    Understand how firewalls, VPNs, HTTPS work, and secure network architecture. Learn about ports, protocols, and how to detect suspicious network activity.

  • 4

    Advanced Threat Analysis Weeks 8-12

    Master malware analysis, digital forensics, log analysis, and incident response. Learn to identify indicators of compromise (IoC) and attack patterns.
